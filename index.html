Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Do students need prior biology or science background?
Absolutely not. This program welcomes students from all backgrounds‚Äîhumanities, arts, sciences, or engineering. We begin with foundational concepts and progressively build complexity. What matters most is intellectual curiosity and willingness to engage with new ideas.
How is laboratory safety ensured?
Our facility complies with all biosafety regulations, and all activities occur under professional supervision. Before any hands-on work, students complete comprehensive safety training covering proper protocols, protective equipment, material handling, and emergency procedures. All biological materials are carefully vetted for safety.
What is the cohort size?
To ensure quality instruction while maintaining collaborative dynamics, we accept cohorts of 20-25 students. This size enables personalized guidance while fostering rich peer discussion and team-based project work.
What tangible outcomes will students produce?
Students will complete at least one comprehensive biodesign project, including research documentation, experimental records, and final presentation materials. These deliverables are suitable for university applications, science competitions, or personal portfolios. More importantly, students develop transdisciplinary thinking, scientific literacy, and innovation capabilities.
How does this relate to iGEM or synthetic biology competitions?
This program employs a project-based learning model similar to iGEM (International Genetically Engineered Machine competition), guiding students through the complete research cycle: problem identification, experimental design, implementation, and presentation. While not directly entering iGEM, students gain equivalent experience and skills, establishing a strong foundation for future international competition participation.
